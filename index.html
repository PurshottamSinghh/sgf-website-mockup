Student Green Fund

Projects and Implementation

Projects Funded by the Student Green Fund

Explore our student-led initiatives organized by semester. These projects are funded by the Student Green Fund and drive sustainability across the UToledo campus.

Ongoing Initiatives

Health Science Tech Park Solar Field (Phase I & II)

In Progress

Description: The Health Science Campus Tech Park Solar Field is a flagship initiative demonstrating UToledo's commitment to renewable energy. Following the successful initial 3.3-acre installation, SGF recently approved a massive $187,000 Phase II expansion. This project generates renewable electricity, reduces campus reliance on fossil fuels, and acts as an active educational resource for engineering and environmental science students.

Spring 2025

UToledo Dining Composting System

Completed

Description: Designed by a Mechanical Engineering Senior Design team, this project implemented a solar-powered composting infrastructure to successfully divert pre-consumer organic food waste from campus dining halls away from local landfills.

Center for Performing Arts Recital Hall LED Conversion

Completed

Description: A comprehensive energy-efficiency upgrade replacing outdated, high-wattage stage lighting in the PA Recital Hall with modern, low-emission LED fixtures.

Savage Business Hydration Station

Completed

Description: Installation of a high-efficiency water bottle refill station within the Savage Business complex, continuing SGF's mission to eliminate single-use plastics across all academic buildings.

Earth Fest Sustainable Food Trucks

Completed

Description: Funding provided to local, sustainable food truck vendors (including Baba's Eats and Susie Lynne's Lemonade) to cater Earth Fest 2025 without utilizing single-use plastics.

Spring 2024

YSI Water Quality Monitoring

Completed

Description: SGF funded advanced YSI water quality probes for graduate and undergraduate researchers to continuously monitor the ecological health of the Ottawa River flowing through Main Campus.

Bumblebee Diversity Project

Completed

Description: A student-led ecological study focusing on local pollinator biodiversity. The project funds research equipment to track and support native bumblebee populations across UToledo's green spaces.

Theatre Stage Lighting Design Upgrade

Completed

Description: Initiated by students in the Stage Lighting Design class, this project replaced legacy, energy-heavy lighting interfaces and fixtures in the Theatre department with sustainable LED technology.

Fall 2023

Stranahan Arboretum Picnic Tables

Completed

Description: Enhancing UToledo's 47-acre Stranahan Arboretum by installing durable, eco-friendly picnic tables to encourage outdoor student learning and community engagement with nature.

CPA Film Department Lighting Grid

Completed

Description: A sustainability upgrade for the Film Department replacing outdated studio instruments in Room 1039 with long-lasting, highly efficient LED lighting grids.

2022 Initiatives

CPA Studio Theatre LED Conversion (Fall 2022)

Completed

Description: A massive $31,000 overhaul of the Center for Performing Arts Studio Theatre, transitioning the entire performance space to an energy-efficient LED ecosystem.

Campus Picnic Tables (Spring 2022)

Completed

Description: SGF funded the widespread installation of outdoor seating across Main Campus to promote outdoor study and appreciation of the campus's natural environment.

Flatlands Hammock Park (Spring 2022)

Completed

Description: Designed to create a relaxing, eco-friendly outdoor space in the Flatlands, this project installed structural posts to allow students to safely set up hammocks without damaging campus trees.

Historical Highlights (2016 - 2018)

Students for Solar (Fall 2018)

Completed

Description: One of the largest grants in SGF history ($300,000), spearheaded by BOSEF to vastly expand the university's solar infrastructure and renewable energy portfolio.

Main Campus Bike Infrastructure (Spring 2017)

Completed

Description: A comprehensive $151,000 overhaul of campus transportation, funding new bike racks, secured corrals, and infrastructure to support the Rocket Wheels bike-share program.

Bike Repair Stations (Summer 2016)

Completed

Description: Installation of standalone bike repair kiosks equipped with tools and air pumps near the library and Palmer Hall to empower sustainable student commuting.

High-Efficiency Hand Dryers (Summer 2016)

Completed

Description: SGF funded the installation of 10 Dyson Airblade hand dryers to replace traditional paper towels, drastically cutting physical waste and minimizing carbon emissions across three campuses.

Law School Cutbank River Path (Fall 2016)

Completed

Description: A $15,000 environmental restoration project to build a sustainable pedestrian path and bench seating along the Ottawa River near the College of Law.

Solar Charging Tables (Spring 2016)

Completed

Description: Installation of three outdoor tables equipped with solar-paneled umbrellas near the Student Union and Engineering campuses, allowing students to charge devices using 100% clean energy.

Public Recycling Cabinets (Spring 2016)

Completed

Description: Funded the purchase of 12 large-capacity, multi-stream recycling cabinets placed in high-traffic outdoor areas to improve campus waste diversion.

GUTS: Greening UToledo Through Service Learning (Summer 2016)

Completed

Description: A hands-on educational initiative that funded the purchase and planting of native seeds and vegetation across campus landscaping beds to restore natural prairie ecosystems.

Bowman-Oddy Outdoor Learning Courtyard (Spring 2016)

Completed

Description: Led by the Senior Environmental Capstone class, this project revitalized the BO courtyard by removing invasive species and planting native flora to create an outdoor ecology classroom.

Foundational Projects (2012 - 2015)

Toledo Filament Recycling Center (Fall 2015)

Completed

Description: Created by the UToledo Maker Society, this project established an innovative recycling center to break down and reuse 3D printer plastics generated by engineering students.

Scenic Reach 5 Ottawa River Path (Spring 2015)

Completed

Description: One of the earliest SGF ecology grants, providing $10,000 to clear invasive species and build sustainable pedestrian access along the scenic Reach 5 section of the Ottawa River.

"Take Back the Tap" Hydration Stations (2012 - 2014)

Completed

Description: Over multiple semesters, SGF partnered with Student Government to fund UToledo's very first high-efficiency, refillable water bottle stations. This foundational investment sparked a campus-wide cultural shift away from single-use plastics.

Earth Fest Bottle and Bag Swap (Spring 2013)

Completed

Description: An early awareness campaign where SGF distributed high-quality reusable bags and aluminum bottles to students in exchange for their disposable plastics to demonstrate the ease of sustainable habits.
